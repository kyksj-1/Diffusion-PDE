\begin{abstract}
Generative diffusion in function space has achieved striking accuracy on smooth {PDE} benchmarks, yet hyperbolic conservation laws remain untouched---the empirical, analytical, and algorithmic blind spot of the paradigm. Central-difference evaluation of {PDE}-residual guidance terms, the workhorse of the current state of the art, becomes singular at the discontinuity interfaces that define the hyperbolic regime.
We resolve this impasse by observing that the score field of a {VE-SDE} diffusion model itself satisfies a viscous Burgers equation---a recent identity that, when the target {PDE} is also of Burgers type, creates two coupled Burgers structures in orthogonal directions. Physical shocks determine the singular support of the data distribution and thereby pin the score-level interfacial layer, forming a geometric correspondence that we formalise as a double-Burgers coupling theorem.
Converting this structural observation into an algorithm, we embed the exact $\tanh$ interfacial profile of the score field directly into the network architecture, decomposing the learned score into a smooth background potential, a signed-distance-to-shock function, and a Rankine--Hugoniot--admissible jump amplitude. This hard-coded architectural prior eliminates the $\exp(\Lambda T_{d})$ error-amplification factor that plagues standard denoisers near shocks, degrading the Gronwall constant in the convergence analysis from $\Lambda$ to $\mathcal{O}(1)$.
The resulting sampler, \emph{EntroDiff}, carries a composite loss family in which each regulariser targets a distinct hypothesis in the convergence proof, and a Godunov-form {PDE} guidance term---entropy-consistent and well-defined across arbitrary jumps---replaces the singular central-difference residual of prior work. Under the BV-aware parameterization and a total-variation penalty that enforces $L^{1}$-compactness, we prove that generated samples converge to the Kruzhkov entropy solution at rate $\Wass{1}\le\mathcal{O}(\varepsilon^{1/2})$ in the score-matching error budget $\varepsilon^{2}$, with constants independent of the amplification exponent. Toy experiments on the inviscid Burgers equation validate the mechanism: the architectural prior enables effective sampling with substantially fewer integration steps than the baseline, and each ablation removes a measurable piece of the empirical margin.
\end{abstract}
